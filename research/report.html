07 · Why Sites Fail

The pitfalls the winners avoid.

The two discount giants we tried to capture (perfume.com, fragrancex.com) were both behind bot-walls — fitting, since their cluttered, dated design is exactly the anti-pattern. Below: three archetypes of weak fragrance sites, and the do/don't they teach.

✕ What kills a perfume site

  • Treating bottles as SKUs in a dense grid — no story, no world
  • Stock photography and a default theme — zero brand identity
  • Notes hidden in a spec table instead of an olfactive arc
  • Fake urgency timers & "12 people viewing" — trust poison
  • Hero that looks nice but buries price, notes and add-to-cart
  • No sampling, no real reviews, no guarantee — all risk on the buyer

✓ What the winners do instead

  • Sell a world first — origin, mood, a named maker
  • Original art-directed macro photography, one restrained palette
  • Notes as a structured pyramid / temporal arc + descriptor tags
  • Concrete proof: star average + count + verified testimonials
  • Cinematic hero with ONE clear CTA and visible price/CTA on PDP
  • De-risk: try-at-home with credit, 30-day guarantee, free-ship
